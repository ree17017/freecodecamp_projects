Introduction

Creating your first character shiould be fun and exciting. Don't stress if it perfect or not this will not be the only character you build.

Pick a name and maybe write a background for your caracter. Keep your background to a few pages unless you have RPGed this out with your DM.

Race Selection

Read though each race description and pick something that sounds like you. It is better to start with something you like or know and then build into more difficult races as you gain more skill.

It can be a lot of fun to pick a non-standard race. Word of advice. If this is your first character pick a main line race.

Personality

Give your character two personality traits. Personality traits are small, simple ways to help you set your character apart from every other character. Your personality traits should tell you something interesting and fun about your character. They should be self descriptions that are specific about what makes your character stand out. “I’m sm art” is not a good trait, because it describes a lot of characters. “I’ve read every book in Candlekeep” tells you something specific about your character’s interests and disposition. Personality traits might describe the things your character likes, his or her past accomplishments, things your character dislikes or fears, your character’s self attitude or mannerism s, or the influence o f his or her ability scores.

To make it easy roll a d8 for your personality trait

    d8 Personality Trait
  • 1 I hide scraps of food and trinkets away in my pockets.
  • 2 I ask a lot of questions.
  • 3 I like to squeeze into small places where no one else can get to me.
  • 4 I sleep with my back to a wall or tree, with everything I own wrapped in a bundle in my arms.
  • 5 I eat like a pig and have bad manners.
  • 6 I think anyone who’s nice to me is hiding evil intent.
  • 7 I don’t like to bathe.
  • 8 I bluntly say what other people are hinting at or hiding

Flaw

Finally, choose a flaw for your character. Your character’s flaw represents som e vice, com pulsion, fear, or w eakness—in particular, anything that som eone else could exploit to bring you to ruin or cause you to act against your best interests. M ore significant than negative personality traits, a flaw might answer any of these questions: W hat enrages you? W hat’s the one person, concept, or event that you are terrified of? What are your vices?

Choosing a flaw can seem hard. But it could be the most played aspect of your character. This could earn you Inspiration points.
